ABOUT ME






Welcome to my think tank! I'm Michelle Pao [/pou/].

I am a sophomore majoring in biomedical engineering and minoring in graphic design at Binghamton University. My future career aspiration is to be a UI/UX designer while being a freelance graphic designer.


Bullet journaling and hand lettering are just some ways I like to get my creative juices flowing. If I'm not creating, you can catch me ordering pink drinks one after another at Starbucks or thinking of ways to expand my portfolio. I am hoping to sell enamel pins soon, so keep your eye out for some swag for your jean jackets, backpacks and cork boards gallore.



I recently joined the Society of Asian Scientists and Engineers. This club lets me interact with the Asian Student Body more, form great connections and gain professionalism through events such as resume building and elevator pitch practice.

This semester, I am an intern for the Binghamton Student Design Agency. I have learned how to make more professional designs and balance these designs from being too playful to too rigid. Some organizations that I have done work for is the Bing Crew Team, Newman House, and BSDA itself!

In January 2019, I became a teaching instructor for Girls Who Code. I have been teaching high school girls around the Binghamton area how to code in Python as well as educating them on female pioneers in code. I have also created posters for this institution and am looking forward to garnering more interest from undergraduate students at Binghamton University to participate in this movement.

In August 2017, I started attending Binghamton University in upstate New York. This decision came from the desire to take a break from the bustling city of Queens and take advantage of Binghamton's history of engineering inventions.
